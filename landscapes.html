Digital Landscapes

motion design
(2023)

This project addresses digital colonisation through a conceptual, research-driven approach, aiming to spark discussion within the evolving landscape of design education.

To mitigate the exclusion inherent in the issues explored, I focused on simplifying dense technical jargon into accessible information. Motion design emerged as the ideal medium for this approach, combining text, sound, and imagery to cater to diverse learning styles.

The result is an immersive digital narrative that blurs the boundaries between natural and artificial, traditional and contemporary, physical and digital. It creates a reflective space for designers to consider their roles in addressing ethical and environmental issues within their practice.
